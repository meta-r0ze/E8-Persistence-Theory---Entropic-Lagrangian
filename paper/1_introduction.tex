\section{Introduction}

In \textit{Informational Energetics: A Universal Architecture of Persistence} \cite{meyer_informational_2026}, we introduced Informational Energetics (IE) and established that any persistent system must resist entropic decay by satisfying six architectural pillars derived from control theory, information theory, and thermodynamics: Capacity (the vessel), Map (the model), Protocol (the interface), Governor (the constraint), Toll (the temporal cost), and Margin (the buffer). That work derived the static architecture of persistence, what a system must \textit{be}. This paper derives the necessary complement, how a persistent system must \textit{behave}, the dynamical rules that its substrate must enforce to maintain coherence over time. The central imperative of Informational Energetics is the \textbf{Selection Principle}: \textit{Among all configurations capable of encoding a given structural complexity, only those that minimize Entropic Action remain observable.} The Entropic Action $S_\Phi$ quantifies the total thermodynamic cost for a system to maintain structural coherence against environmental entropy over its persistence lifetime. In \cref{sec:entropic_action}, we formalize this cost and demonstrate that the Principle of Least Action is not an independent axiom of mechanics but the strict thermodynamic condition of minimum entropy production required for a system to survive.

\subsection{The Falsification Test: The Vacuum Substrate}
To validate this universal architecture, we apply it to the most demanding test case: the quantum vacuum. While open systems (such as biological organisms) can offset their action cost by maintaining a non-equilibrium steady state through a continuous flux of energy and matter, the vacuum possesses no external environment. To persist rather than dissolving into thermal noise, its optimization criterion is absolute: it must strictly minimize its internal rate of dissipation.

Applying this absolute thermodynamic constraint provides a structural solution to a longstanding conceptual puzzle: the origin of the Standard Model Lagrangian. While it is one of the most successful mathematical structures in the history of science, standard Quantum Field Theory (QFT) cannot explain \textit{why} nature chooses this specific form. It postulates the gauge symmetries, enforces renormalizability as a mathematical requirement rather than a physical limit, and inserts the parameter set $\mathcal{P} = \{g, \lambda, m, v\}$ entirely by hand.

Because information is physical, every state transition on this substrate carries an irreducible thermodynamic cost. Because the six pillars encompass both internal field dynamics and spacetime geometry, the resulting \textbf{Entropic Lagrangian} unifies the Standard Model with General Relativity in a single variational principle.

\subsubsection{Structure of the Derivation}
\Cref{sec:entropic_lagrangian} maps the Entropic Action to the $E_8$ substrate, formulating the covariant action $S_\Phi = \int d^4x \sqrt{-g} \mathcal{L}_\Phi$, defining kinematic and thermodynamic bounds, and translating discrete information-theoretic costs into continuous field operators.

The resulting structure is closed; each pillar mandates a specific term in the Lagrangian, leaving no freedom for arbitrary additions or omissions. \Cref{sec:map_capacity,sec:protocol,sec:governor,sec:toll_margin} systematically derive the field equations for each architectural pillar.

This structural derivation carries profound conceptual consequences absent from standard QFT: a physical origin for the Matter/Force distinction, a structural explanation for UV completion, and rigid, falsifiable predictions (\cref{sec:falsifiability}).
