\begin{abstract}
Any system that persists against entropy must minimize its Entropic Action, the irreversible computational drag of maintaining structural coherence. We define this action fundamentally as the product of a state transtion cost, system's structural complexity and its transition flux. The Principle of Least Action emerges not as an irreducible axiom, but as the necessary condition of Minimum Entropy Production required for a discrete substrate to resist dissolution.

Applying this principle to the $E_8$ lattice substrate derived in the companion paper, we define the Entropic Action as the physical thermodynamic dual to the Euclidean Action of field theory. We demonstrate that optimizing this action against the $E_8$ lattice to satisfy the six pillars of persistence uniquely yields the operators of the Standard Model and General Relativity: the fermion mass term (Capacity), the Dirac operator (Map) governs chiral propagation; Yang-Mills gauge fields (Protocol) penalize phase decoherence; the Higgs potential (Governor) enforces finite channel capacity; and the Einstein-Hilbert action (Toll and Margin) manifests as the bulk cost of updating causal geometry. These structures are not postulated, but derived as the unique architectural requirements for existence.
\end{abstract}
