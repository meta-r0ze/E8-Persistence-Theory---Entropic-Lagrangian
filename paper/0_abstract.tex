\begin{abstract}
Any system that persists against entropy must minimize its Entropic Action, the irreversible computational drag of maintaining structural coherence. We define this action fundamentally as the time-integrated product of a system's state transition cost, structural complexity, and kinematic flux. Consequently, the Principle of Least Action emerges not as an irreducible axiom of mechanics, but as the necessary thermodynamic condition of Minimum Entropy Production required for a system to resist dissolution.

We demonstrate that optimizing this action to satisfy the six pillars of persistence uniquely yielding a unified variational principle for the Standard Model and General Relativity, with the specific gauge structures and coupling constants determined by the discrete substrate geometry (derived in companion work). By enforcing local hardware limits (bandwidth and channel capacity), the architecture strictly dictates the required field operators: Capacity requires the fermion mass term; Map requires the Dirac operator for chiral propagation; Protocol necessitates Yang-Mills gauge fields to penalize phase decoherence; Governor demands the Higgs potential to prevent causal aliasing; and Toll and Margin mandate the Einstein-Hilbert action as the bulk metabolic cost of spacetime geometry. 

Because the finite capacity of the lattice mathematically exhausts the allowed operator space, the Entropic Lagrangian is structurally closed. These structures are not postulated to fit empirical data, but derived as the unique, unavoidable architectural requirements for existence.
\end{abstract}
